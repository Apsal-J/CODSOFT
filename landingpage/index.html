About Puma

Puma is a renowned German multinational corporation that designs and manufactures athletic and casual footwear, apparel, and accessories. Established in 1948 by Rudolf Dassler, the company has grown into one of the world's leading sports brands, known for its innovative designs and commitment to quality. Puma's product range caters to various sports, including football, running, training, and motorsports, as well as lifestyle products that blend sportswear with fashion.

Our Sport

Puma has a rich history of involvement in various sports, sponsoring numerous athletes and teams across the globe. In athletics, as of February 2025, Puma sponsors several national Olympic committees and athletics federations, including those of the Bahamas, Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, Cuba, Portugal, and Switzerland. Notable athletes such as Karsten Warholm, Shericka Jackson, Mondo Duplantis, and Elaine Thompson-Herah have achieved remarkable feats wearing Puma gear. In football, Puma has been the official kit supplier for various national teams and club sides. The brand's influence extends to motorsports, where it partners with prominent Formula 1 teams like Mercedes-AMG Petronas and Scuderia Ferrari, providing them with race suits, shoes, and other gear. Puma's commitment to motorsports was further solidified in 2024 when it became the official Formula 1 trackside vendor and personnel supplier, and also an official partner of the F1 Academy.
